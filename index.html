The Jim Twins Project

Exploring the intersection of Genetics, Neuroscience, and Determinism.

Case Study: The Jim Twins

Jim Lewis and Jim Springer were identical twins separated at birth and reunited at age 39. Despite living apart, their lives were nearly identical mirrors:

  • Both named James by adoptive parents.
  • Both married women named Linda, then Betty.
  • Both named their sons James Alan (or Allan).
  • Both drove the same car, smoked the same cigarettes, and worked in law enforcement.

Is this coincidence, or is our "free will" a biological script?

The Neuroscience of Choice

Modern research suggests the brain initiates decisions up to 7-10 seconds before we are consciously aware of them. This "Readiness Potential" challenges the idea that we are the conscious authors of our actions.
